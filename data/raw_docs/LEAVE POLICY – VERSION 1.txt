LEAVE POLICY – VERSION 1

1. PURPOSE
This policy defines the rules related to leave eligibility, approval, leave categories, documentation requirements, carry-forward rules, and absence handling for full-time employees and interns. The purpose of the policy is to ensure fair leave administration while maintaining business continuity and clear communication between employees, reporting managers, and the HR team.

2. APPLICABILITY
This policy applies to:
- Full-time employees
- Probationary employees
- Interns, to the extent specifically stated in this policy or in their internship agreement

Consultants, contractors, and temporary third-party staff are not automatically covered under this leave policy unless their engagement contract explicitly refers to it.

3. GENERAL LEAVE PRINCIPLES
3.1 Leave must be requested through the company leave portal.
3.2 Except in emergency situations, leave should be applied for in advance and approved before the employee is absent from work.
3.3 Employees are responsible for ensuring a proper handover of urgent work before proceeding on leave.
3.4 Approval of leave depends on business requirements, staffing availability, project deadlines, and manager discretion.
3.5 HR may request supporting documents in case of long leave, repeated unplanned leave, or suspected misuse of leave.

4. PLANNED LEAVE
4.1 Planned leave refers to leave that can reasonably be scheduled in advance, such as personal work, family functions, travel, or appointments.
4.2 Employees must submit planned leave requests at least 3 working days in advance through the company leave portal.
4.3 Planned leave is considered approved only after the reporting manager approves it in the system.
4.4 If the leave request is urgent but still foreseeable, the employee should notify the reporting manager directly in addition to raising the request in the portal.
4.5 In case of overlapping leave requests within the same team, the reporting manager may stagger approvals based on project needs.

5. SICK LEAVE
5.1 Sick leave may be availed in case of genuine illness, injury, or medical incapacity that prevents the employee from performing work.
5.2 Employees should inform their reporting manager as early as possible on the day of absence.
5.3 If sick leave extends beyond 2 consecutive working days, the employee must submit a medical certificate within 48 hours of returning to work.
5.4 Repeated short-duration sick leave patterns may be reviewed by HR if they indicate possible misuse.
5.5 Sick leave may be denied if the employee fails to provide requested medical documentation in cases where documentation is mandatory.

6. CASUAL LEAVE
6.1 Employees are eligible for 6 casual leave days per calendar year.
6.2 Casual leave is intended for short personal needs, urgent domestic matters, or unavoidable commitments.
6.3 Casual leave cannot be carried forward to the next calendar year.
6.4 Casual leave cannot normally be taken for more than 3 consecutive working days unless approved by HR and the reporting manager.

7. EARNED LEAVE
7.1 Employees are eligible for 12 earned leave days per calendar year.
7.2 Earned leave may be used for planned time off, family travel, rest, or personal commitments.
7.3 A maximum of 5 earned leave days may be carried forward into the next calendar year.
7.4 Earned leave encashment, if any, will be governed by separate payroll or employment rules and is not guaranteed under this policy.
7.5 Employees are encouraged to plan earned leave in advance to avoid year-end congestion.

8. LEAVE DURING PROBATION
8.1 Employees on probation may avail sick leave and casual leave subject to manager approval.
8.2 Earned leave can only be availed after successful completion of the probation period.
8.3 Frequent absence during probation may be considered during confirmation review if it materially affects performance or training.

9. INTERNSHIP LEAVE
9.1 Interns are eligible for up to 2 paid leave days per month, subject to prior manager approval.
9.2 Any leave beyond this may be treated as unpaid unless specifically approved by HR or covered by the internship agreement.
9.3 Interns must inform both their mentor and reporting manager when seeking leave.
9.4 Extended unapproved absence may affect internship evaluation and certificate issuance.

10. LEAVE DURING CRITICAL PROJECT PERIODS
10.1 During major releases, audits, customer go-live periods, or business-critical review windows, managers may request employees to avoid non-essential leave.
10.2 Leave may still be approved in emergencies, medical situations, or compelling personal circumstances.
10.3 Employees are encouraged to discuss long leave plans early if a project deadline is approaching.

11. ABSENCE WITHOUT APPROVAL
11.1 Absence without manager intimation for a full working day may be treated as unauthorized absence.
11.2 Unapproved absence for more than 2 consecutive working days may be treated as misconduct and escalated to HR.
11.3 Salary deduction, warning letters, or further disciplinary review may apply depending on the seriousness of the case and prior history.

12. HOLIDAY ADJACENT LEAVE
12.1 Employees are expected to apply in advance for leave adjacent to public holidays or weekends.
12.2 Managers may restrict overlapping long weekends if business coverage is affected.
12.3 Abuse of holiday-adjacent leave patterns may be reviewed by HR.

13. DOCUMENTATION AND RECORD KEEPING
13.1 HR maintains leave balances and leave records through the leave management system.
13.2 Employees are responsible for checking the status of their leave requests and ensuring approvals are reflected correctly.
13.3 In case of a system issue, HR may process leave manually based on email approval records.

14. POLICY INTERPRETATION
Any exception to this policy requires approval from HR and the relevant business manager. In case of ambiguity, the HR team’s interpretation of the leave policy will prevail unless a statutory requirement states otherwise.
