GRIEVANCE POLICY

1. PURPOSE
This policy defines the process through which employees and interns may raise workplace concerns, complaints, or grievances in a fair, confidential, and structured manner. The policy is intended to promote timely resolution of concerns while protecting individuals from retaliation.

2. SCOPE
Grievances may relate to:
- workplace behavior
- harassment or inappropriate conduct
- unfair treatment
- leave disputes
- attendance disputes
- reimbursement concerns
- workload concerns
- manager conduct
- internship-related concerns
- policy implementation disputes

This policy is not a substitute for statutory complaint procedures where a separate legal mechanism applies, but HR may guide employees to the appropriate channel.

3. WHO CAN RAISE A GRIEVANCE
3.1 Employees may raise a grievance under this policy.
3.2 Interns may also use this grievance mechanism for workplace concerns, mentor issues, unfair treatment, or internship-related disputes.
3.3 Where a person is uncomfortable speaking directly to the manager involved, they may approach HR directly.

4. FIRST-LEVEL RESOLUTION
4.1 Employees and interns are encouraged to first raise the issue with the reporting manager or mentor where appropriate, unless the grievance directly involves that person.
4.2 First-level resolution is intended for issues that can be addressed quickly through clarification, discussion, or immediate corrective action.
4.3 No person is required to raise a grievance with the person who is the subject of the grievance.

5. HR ESCALATION
5.1 If the grievance is not resolved at the first level, or if the employee is uncomfortable raising it with the manager, the grievance may be escalated to HR through email or the designated grievance form.
5.2 HR may acknowledge the complaint, collect basic details, and decide the next review steps.

6. INVESTIGATION PROCESS
6.1 HR may conduct a preliminary review to understand the nature of the grievance.
6.2 HR may speak with the concerned parties, gather relevant records, review policy documents, and decide whether a formal investigation is required.
6.3 Where needed, HR may involve business leadership or another neutral reviewer while preserving confidentiality as far as possible.

7. CONFIDENTIALITY
7.1 Grievance matters should be handled confidentially to the extent reasonably possible while allowing a fair review of the issue.
7.2 Information will be shared only with those who need to know for investigation or resolution purposes.

8. PROTECTION FROM RETALIATION
8.1 No employee or intern should face retaliation for raising a grievance in good faith.
8.2 Retaliation, intimidation, or victimization of a complainant or witness may itself lead to disciplinary action.

9. RESOLUTION TIMELINE
9.1 HR aims to acknowledge a grievance within 2 working days.
9.2 HR aims to provide an initial response within 7 working days, although complex matters may require additional time depending on evidence collection and stakeholder availability.
9.3 The complainant may be updated if the matter requires a longer investigation period.

10. RECORD KEEPING
10.1 HR will maintain grievance records, key actions taken, and closure notes in accordance with internal record-keeping practices.
10.2 Access to grievance records is restricted to authorized personnel.

11. FALSE OR MALICIOUS COMPLAINTS
11.1 Good-faith complaints are protected even if the grievance is not ultimately substantiated.
11.2 Deliberately false, fabricated, or malicious complaints may be reviewed separately and may attract disciplinary action if bad faith is established.

12. POLICY INTERPRETATION
Questions regarding this policy may be directed to HR. HR may issue clarifications on procedure, timelines, or escalation handling where needed.
