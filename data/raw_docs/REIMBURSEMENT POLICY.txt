REIMBURSEMENT POLICY

1. PURPOSE
This policy defines reimbursement rules for approved business expenses, internet support for remote work, travel claims, documentation requirements, claim timelines, and exceptions. The objective is to ensure that legitimate business expenses are reimbursed in a timely and auditable manner while preventing misuse.

2. APPLICABILITY
This policy applies to employees unless otherwise restricted by role, employment type, project budget, or finance approval conditions. Intern reimbursement is governed by a separate section of this policy and may also depend on the internship agreement.

3. ELIGIBLE REIMBURSEMENT CATEGORIES
Employees may claim reimbursement for:
- approved business travel
- internet expenses for remote or hybrid work
- approved project-related purchases
- local conveyance during authorized business travel
- approved client meeting expenses where explicitly permitted

4. INTERNET REIMBURSEMENT
4.1 Employees working remotely or in hybrid mode are eligible for internet reimbursement up to INR 900 per month upon submission of valid bills.
4.2 Reimbursement is limited to the actual paid amount or the approved cap, whichever is lower.
4.3 Bills must clearly show the billing period, service provider, and payment amount.
4.4 Employees are expected to submit internet claims within the standard reimbursement submission timeline.

5. TRAVEL REIMBURSEMENT
5.1 Travel expenses for official work are reimbursable only if pre-approved by the reporting manager or business owner.
5.2 Original bills or valid digital invoices must be submitted within 15 days of travel completion.
5.3 Travel by air, train, taxi, or local transport should be reasonable and aligned with business need and applicable travel norms.
5.4 Luxury or premium expenses beyond approved travel norms may be partially reimbursed or rejected.

6. FOOD AND LOCAL CONVEYANCE
6.1 Food and local conveyance expenses incurred during approved business travel may be reimbursed subject to bill submission and manager approval.
6.2 Routine daily commute expenses between home and office are not reimbursable unless explicitly approved for a business purpose.

7. PROJECT PURCHASES
7.1 Employees may claim reimbursement for project-related purchases only if they were approved in advance by the reporting manager or budget owner.
7.2 Unapproved purchases, even if work-related, may be rejected by finance.

8. INTERN REIMBURSEMENT
8.1 Interns are not automatically eligible for reimbursement.
8.2 Reimbursement for interns is allowed only if:
- the internship agreement explicitly provides for it, or
- the manager has approved a project-specific claim in writing, or
- HR and finance have approved a specific exception
8.3 Interns should not assume internet or travel reimbursement eligibility unless it has been clearly communicated.

9. INELIGIBLE CLAIMS
The following are not eligible for reimbursement unless specifically approved in writing:
- personal expenses
- entertainment expenses
- penalties or fines
- unapproved purchases
- expenses lacking valid supporting bills
- routine home-to-office commute

10. CLAIM SUBMISSION TIMELINE
10.1 All reimbursement claims must be submitted within 30 calendar days from the date of the expense unless an exception is approved by finance.
10.2 Delayed claims may be rejected if the delay is not justified.

11. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
11.1 Every reimbursement claim must include:
- expense date
- category of expense
- amount claimed
- supporting bill or invoice
- business justification where required
11.2 Finance may request additional clarification or supporting records before approving a claim.

12. FRAUD OR MISUSE
Submitting false bills, duplicate claims, inflated claims, or manipulated invoices may result in claim rejection, recovery action, disciplinary proceedings, or other action under company policy.
