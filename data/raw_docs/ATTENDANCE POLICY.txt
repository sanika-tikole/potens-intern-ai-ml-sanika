ATTENDANCE POLICY

1. PURPOSE
This policy defines attendance expectations, working hours, attendance marking procedures, remote work attendance obligations, late-arrival handling, and consequences of repeated attendance irregularities. The purpose is to maintain operational discipline while supporting fair attendance practices across employees and interns.

2. APPLICABILITY
This policy applies to all employees and interns unless a separate shift policy or project-specific attendance arrangement has been approved in writing.

3. STANDARD WORKING HOURS
3.1 Standard office working hours are 9:30 AM to 6:30 PM from Monday to Friday.
3.2 Employees are expected to be available during core collaboration hours from 11:00 AM to 4:00 PM.
3.3 Managers may approve flexibility in start or end times depending on business requirements, shift allocation, or medical accommodation.

4. ATTENDANCE RECORDING
4.1 All employees and interns must mark attendance using the designated attendance management system on each working day.
4.2 If the attendance system is unavailable due to a technical issue, the employee must inform the reporting manager and HR on the same day.
4.3 Proxy attendance, fraudulent attendance marking, or deliberate manipulation of attendance records may lead to disciplinary action.

5. LATE ARRIVAL
5.1 Employees arriving more than 15 minutes late on three occasions within a calendar month may receive a verbal or written warning from HR or the reporting manager.
5.2 Persistent late arrival may affect performance review, team reliability assessment, or manager feedback.
5.3 Repeated lateness without valid reason may be treated as attendance misconduct.

6. EARLY DEPARTURE
6.1 Employees leaving before the scheduled end of working hours should inform the reporting manager in advance where possible.
6.2 Repeated early departure without approval may be treated as an attendance irregularity.
6.3 Where early departure is required for personal emergencies, employees should regularize the time through leave or manager approval if required.

7. MINIMUM ATTENDANCE FOR INTERNS
7.1 Interns are expected to maintain at least 85 percent attendance during the internship period unless a different threshold is stated in the internship offer letter.
7.2 Extended absence without prior approval may affect internship evaluation, stipend processing, extension decisions, and certificate eligibility.
7.3 The mentor and HR may review intern attendance at periodic checkpoints.

8. REMOTE WORK ATTENDANCE
8.1 Employees working remotely or in hybrid mode must remain available on the approved company communication platform during working hours.
8.2 Employees must update task status before the end of the day if required by their team process.
8.3 Repeated non-availability during working hours without prior notice may be treated as an attendance issue.

9. ABSENCE WITHOUT INTIMATION
9.1 Failure to inform the reporting manager of absence for a full working day may be treated as unauthorized absence.
9.2 Repeated absence without intimation may lead to counseling, warning, or HR escalation depending on the seriousness and pattern.

10. BIOMETRIC OR SYSTEM COMPLIANCE
10.1 Where the organization uses biometric attendance, employees must follow the prescribed process for punch-in and punch-out.
10.2 Manual attendance correction requests may require manager approval and supporting explanation.
10.3 Deliberate falsification of attendance correction requests may be treated as misconduct.

11. ATTENDANCE REVIEW
11.1 HR and managers may periodically review attendance records for patterns of late arrival, absenteeism, unexplained gaps, or system non-compliance.
11.2 Repeated attendance irregularities may result in:
- counseling
- formal warning
- salary deduction where applicable
- escalation under disciplinary procedure

12. EXCEPTIONS
Attendance exceptions may be granted in medical, family emergency, accessibility, travel, or project-specific cases if approved by the manager and HR where required.
