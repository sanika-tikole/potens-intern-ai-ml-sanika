LEAVE POLICY – VERSION 2

1. PURPOSE
This revised leave policy defines leave eligibility, approval rules, leave categories, supporting documentation requirements, and disciplinary consequences related to unauthorized absence. It is intended to provide a structured and transparent framework for employees and interns while aligning leave administration with revised HR governance standards.

2. APPLICABILITY
This policy applies to:
- Full-time employees
- Employees on probation
- Interns, where specifically stated
- Employees on hybrid or remote arrangements, unless a separate arrangement supersedes this policy

Third-party contractors and consultants are not covered unless their contract explicitly adopts this policy.

3. GENERAL LEAVE RULES
3.1 All leave requests must be submitted through the leave management portal.
3.2 Leave should not be treated as approved until it is confirmed in the system by the reporting manager.
3.3 Employees are expected to plan leave responsibly and ensure work handover where required.
3.4 HR may review leave trends, suspicious patterns, or excessive unscheduled leave for compliance purposes.
3.5 Managers may deny or reschedule non-essential leave if project delivery or customer commitments would be adversely affected.

4. PLANNED LEAVE
4.1 Planned leave includes any leave that is foreseeable and can reasonably be scheduled in advance.
4.2 Employees must submit planned leave requests at least 5 working days in advance through the leave management portal.
4.3 Planned leave is approved only after manager confirmation in the system.
4.4 Where a team faces multiple overlapping leave requests, the manager may prioritize business continuity, client commitments, and critical project timelines while deciding approvals.
4.5 Employees are encouraged to discuss long planned leave at least two weeks in advance where feasible.

5. SICK LEAVE
5.1 Sick leave may be availed for genuine illness, injury, or medical incapacity.
5.2 Employees should inform the reporting manager as soon as possible on the day of absence.
5.3 If sick leave extends beyond 3 consecutive working days, the employee must submit a medical certificate to HR within 72 hours of returning to work.
5.4 HR may request additional medical documentation where the leave pattern suggests repeated misuse or where long absence needs verification.
5.5 Sick leave approval may be regularized after submission of supporting medical records where required.

6. CASUAL LEAVE
6.1 Employees are eligible for 6 casual leave days per calendar year.
6.2 Casual leave is intended for urgent personal matters, family needs, or short planned absence.
6.3 Casual leave lapses at the end of the calendar year and cannot be carried forward.
6.4 Casual leave exceeding 2 consecutive working days may require manager justification depending on team workload.

7. EARNED LEAVE
7.1 Employees are eligible for 12 earned leave days per calendar year.
7.2 Earned leave may be used for planned vacations, personal work, family commitments, or recovery periods.
7.3 A maximum of 7 earned leave days may be carried forward into the next calendar year.
7.4 Carry-forward beyond the permitted limit is not allowed unless specifically approved by HR under exceptional circumstances.
7.5 Employees are encouraged to spread earned leave through the year to avoid last-quarter operational pressure.

8. LEAVE DURING PROBATION
8.1 Employees on probation may avail casual leave and sick leave with manager approval.
8.2 Earned leave becomes available only after probation is successfully completed and confirmed by HR.
8.3 Frequent absence during probation may be taken into account during performance review and confirmation decisions.

9. INTERNSHIP LEAVE
9.1 Interns are eligible for 1 paid leave day per month.
9.2 Any additional leave requires approval from both the mentor and HR and may be treated as unpaid depending on internship terms.
9.3 Interns are expected to maintain regular communication with their mentor regarding absence.
9.4 Unapproved absence may affect final evaluation, extension decisions, and certificate eligibility.

10. BUSINESS-CRITICAL PERIODS
10.1 During audit windows, major product releases, customer escalations, or other business-critical periods, managers may limit non-essential leave approvals.
10.2 Emergency, medical, and compassionate cases will continue to be considered separately.
10.3 Employees should coordinate early with the manager if they anticipate leave during a critical project milestone.

11. ABSENCE WITHOUT APPROVAL
11.1 Absence without informing the manager may be treated as unauthorized absence.
11.2 Unapproved absence for more than 3 consecutive working days may trigger HR review and disciplinary action.
11.3 Depending on the circumstances, the company may issue a warning, treat the period as loss of pay, or escalate the matter for misconduct review.

12. LEAVE NEAR HOLIDAYS OR LONG WEEKENDS
12.1 Leave requests around public holidays, festivals, and long weekends should be planned early.
12.2 Managers may regulate approvals where multiple employees from the same team seek leave simultaneously.
12.3 HR may review suspicious attendance patterns around holidays if misuse is suspected.

13. RECORDS AND COMPLIANCE
13.1 Leave balances and approvals are maintained in the HR leave system.
13.2 Employees must verify that their leave status is updated correctly after approval.
13.3 If a technical issue affects the leave portal, email approval from the reporting manager may be used temporarily and regularized later in the system.

14. POLICY INTERPRETATION
The HR department reserves the right to interpret this policy, issue clarifications, and approve exceptions in consultation with business leadership where necessary.
